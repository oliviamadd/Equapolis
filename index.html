App Features

Neighbourhood Equity Index

The Neighbourhood Inequity Index (NEI) provides a snapshot into the quality of life across the 140 neighbourhoods of Toronto. The index is based on five factors: Economic Opportunities, Social Development, Healthy Lives, Participation in Decision-Making, and Physical Surroundings which are inextricably linked to the residents of each neighbourhood’s socioeconomic status and wellbeing.

Search + Compare

Users are able to compare the NEI between different neighbourhoods of their choice to further understand where, and to what extent, disparities exist within Toronto by using the "Search by Neighbourhood" and "Compare Neighbourhoods" tools. Users can even compare different datasets to each other directly using the “Compare Layers” tool, such as the NEI and the concentration of visible minorities living in each neighbourhood.

Visible Minorities

Visible minorities are disproportionately affected by socioeconomic disparity within Toronto. In order to reduce inequality, it is necessary to expose inequality. Because inequality doesn’t take place in a vacuum, this includes taking into account phenomena such as systemic racism.

Food Banks

Lower socioeconomic status is heavily linked with increased food insecurity. The correlation is intuitively logical - with less time and money available, people of lower socioeconomic status don't always have the means to attain food. To make our app useful for people facing these issues, the location of Toronto food banks is included. Users are able to locate the food bank nearest them by inputting their current location using the "Locate Nearby Food Banks" tool.
